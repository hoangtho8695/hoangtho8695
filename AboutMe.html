Overview

From an early stage in my career, I have been drawn to the structure and clarity that numbers bring — the way patterns emerge from complexity, and how insights, when uncovered, can drive meaningful change. This natural inclination toward analysis was first shaped not through code or data models, but through language and learning. As an English Curriculum Manager and Project Assistant in the education sector, I learned to dissect nuanced content, manage cross-functional projects, and navigate the intersection of logic and creativity. I dealt not just with lessons, but with layered systems — ones that required planning, pattern recognition, and constant refinement.

Over time, I realized that the same problem-solving instincts I used to build programs and coordinate initiatives could be amplified through data. My transition into analytics has been both intentional and deeply fulfilling. It has allowed me to extend my curiosity into new domains — from exploring behavioral trends to designing dashboards that inform decision-making.

What drives me today is the excitement of discovery: of asking the right questions, building models that reflect the real world, and translating data into clarity. In my own time, I immerse myself in new tools, techniques, and domains — whether it’s learning the nuances of SQL queries, visualizing business performance, or studying how analytics shapes industries.

I bring with me not just technical skill, but a thoughtful and human-centered approach to data — one that’s grounded in communication, strategy, and a lifelong love for learning.
